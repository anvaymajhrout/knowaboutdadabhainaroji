Dadabhai Naoroji

He was a Liberal Party Member of Parliament (MP) in the United Kingdom House of Commons between 1892 and 1895 and the first Asian to be a British MP.

Naoroji was a staunch advocate for Indian self-rule and a key figure in the early Indian National Congress, which was founded in 1885. His works and efforts in the British Parliament were instrumental in bringing the plight of India under British rule to the forefront of international attention.

He is also known for his work on economic theories, particularly the "Drain Theory," which postulated that Britain was draining India's wealth to the detriment of the Indian economy. Naoroji also served as a professor of mathematics and natural philosophy at Elphinstone College in Bombay and was instrumental in the establishment of several educational institutions.

Key Achievements

  • First Asian to be a British MP.
  • Co-founder of the Indian National Congress.
  • Developed the Drain Theory to highlight the economic exploitation of India by Britain.
  • Advocated for Indian self-rule and constitutional reforms.
  • Authored significant works on Indian economy and politics.
  • Served as a professor at Elphinstone College.
  • Founded the London Indian Society and the East India Association.

Dadabhai Naoroji was born into a Parsi family in Bombay (now Mumbai), where he excelled academically from an early age. He was one of the founders of the Indian National Congress and played a pivotal role in the Swadeshi Movement, promoting the use of Indian goods and boycotting British products.

He was also involved in social reforms, advocating for women's education and the abolition of the caste system. Naoroji's dedication to public service and his efforts in the British Parliament earned him immense respect and admiration in India and abroad.
